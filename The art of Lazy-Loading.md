Inspired by the design of Neovim's plugin system, where plugins are being lazy loaded on the fly, this approach creates a seamless workflow as it reduces the loading time at startup.

This idea is very similar to a coding principle: You Ain't Gonna Need It. It means if you think a feature is probably worth doing later, do not overcomplicate the current code  architecture. Simpler is better, most of the time, especially in this era.

In our daily lives, we also don't need to take in so much information, or to process a task that we think we may need to in the future. Overcomplicating things doesn't help, sometimes doing it later can be more time-efficient and energy-efficient.

However, it's worth noting that it doesn't mean we need to be lazy at all times, when it comes to skill building, gaining experience, or improving our health, it's always worth it to do it right now.
