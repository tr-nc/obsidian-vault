Inspired by the design of neovim plugin system, where plugins are being lazy loaded on the flight, it creates a fluent workflow as it reduces the loading time at startup.

This idea is very similar to a coding principle: You Ain't Gonna Need It; It means if you think a feature is probably worth doing later, do not over complicate the current code  architecture. Simpler is better, most of the times, especially in this era.

In our daily lives, we also don't need to load that much info, or to process a task that we think we may need to in the future. Over complicate things doesn't help, sometimes doing it later can be more time efficient and power efficient.

However, it's worth noticing that it doesn't mean we need to be lazy at all times, when it comes to skill building, experiencing, or making us more healthy, it's always worth it to do it right now.
